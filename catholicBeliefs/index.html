Catholic Beliefs

First of all, in Reconciliation, what we do is confess our sins in front of God and the Church. We express our contrition in a meaningful manner, receive Christ's forgiveness and that of the Church, offer compensation for our actions, and promise to do better from then on.

Reconciliation is the process or act of bringing together persons who are on opposite sides. In Christianity, it is associated with the death of Jesus, which brought man nearer to God. It is also linked with forgiveness since resolution of a disagreement may require one to show forgiveness.

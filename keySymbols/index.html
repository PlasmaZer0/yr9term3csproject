Key Symbols

The five major symbols recognised in the church for reconciliation are Keys, Purple Stole, Raised Hand, Sign of the Cross and a Scourging Whip. The Keys crossed in the shape of an X is the more commonly known symbol for the Sacrament of Reconciliation.

1. The Sacrament of Confession: This sacrament is also known as the Sacrament of Penance or Reconciliation. It involves confession before the priest, obtaining absolution, and doing the penance.


2. The Crucifix: The crucifix is a sign or signal of the very resurrection of Christ in terms of sacrifice and forgiveness. As such, it became a sign or signal for reconciliation between God and humankind.


3. The Sign of the Cross: It is a gesture performed both at the beginning and at the end of confession and signifies the reconciliation of the penitent to God because of the grace from the sacrifice of Christ.


4. The Stole: The use of a stole at confession suggests that the priest acts as an intermediary between God and the penitent.


5. The White Garment: In some traditions, white vestments are worn or used symbolically to express the purity and forgiveness obtained through reconciliation.


6. The Keys: Symbolic of the authority given by Christ to the Church to bind and loose sins according to Matthew 16:19.

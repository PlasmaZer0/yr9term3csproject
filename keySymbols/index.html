Key Symbols

The five major symbols recognised in the church for reconciliation are Keys, Purple Stole, Raised Hand, Sign of the Cross and a Scourging Whip. The Keys crossed in the shape of an X is the more commonly known symbol for the Sacrament of Reconciliation.

1. The Sacrament of Confession: This sacrament, also known as the Sacrament of Penance or Reconciliation, involves confessing sins to a priest, receiving absolution, and performing penance.


2. The Crucifix: The crucifix is a central symbol of Christ’s sacrifice and forgiveness, emphasizing the ultimate act of reconciliation between God and humanity.


3. The Sign of the Cross: This gesture, made at the beginning and end of confession, signifies the reconciliation of the penitent with God through the grace of Christ’s sacrifice.


4. The Stole: The stole worn by the priest during confession symbolizes the priest's role as a mediator between the penitent and God.


5. The White Garment: In some traditions, a white garment may be worn or symbolically used to represent the purity and forgiveness granted through reconciliation.


6. The Keys: Symbolic of the authority given to the Church by Christ to bind and loose sins, based on Matthew 16:19.
